Club Updates and General Meetings

Hello everyone! Our upcoming meeting is scheduled for 6:00 on Monday March 3rd, with the room information to be announced. Keep an eye out for an email and updates on Instagram. We are currently in the process of discussing alternative communication methods to best reach all club members, so more on that in the meeting Monday!

General plans for our meeting this Monday is to once again go over sports news headlines, split into groups once again to further project discussions, and also have mini sports analytics history lesson.

Some more general announcements - the project proposal form is still active, so please fill it out if you come up with any more project ideas! The goal is to get you in your groups and establish a timeline for (hopefully) a presentation at the end of the semester. If you have no coding experience, that is more than okay. Feel free to do a deep dive on a research topic instead! We want you to be able to build your portfolio in a way that best suits your skill set.

We also are still taking applications for those interested in working on the newsletter! Fill out the interest form if you would like to get involved! More to come after spring break.

We are also adding a feedback form to the newsletter. If you have any feedback about the newsletter, the club, the projects, anything at all, please feel free to fill out the feedback form. The form is optionally anonymous and will not collect emails, so do not let that hold you back from providing feedback. We want to hear from you and build a club that people enjoy! Any feedback, good or bad, is appreciated.

Sports Headlines

Canada Wins Four Nations Faceoff

Team Canada won the first ever Four Nations Face Off in an overtime winner from Nathan MacKinnon, beating Team USA 3-2. MacKinnon won MVP of the tournament for his performance.

The contest drew 16.1 million viewers, making it ESPN’s most watched hockey game and Sportsnet’s second most watched. Canada’s goalie, Jordan Binnington, led the tournament with most high danger saves with 29 throughout the tournament.  


MLB Spring Training Is Underway

February 22nd marked the start of spring training for MLB teams in Arizona and Florida. Some stand out performances include Juan Soto, who just signed a record-breaking deal with the New York Mets. He homered his first at bat against the Houston Astros. National League Cy Young Award winner Chris Sale threw two perfect innings against the Twins.

Opening day for the MLB is March 18th, with the LA Dodgers taking on the Chicago Cubs in Tokyo, Japan to play in the Tokyo Dome.
